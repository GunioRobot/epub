 | notes    = Hard Times ''is a novel by Charles Dickens, published in 1854. It is significant for being the shortest of his full novels. The book is one of a number of state-of-the-nation novels published around the same time, another being'' [[North and South]] ''by [[Author:Elizabeth Gaskell|Elizabeth Gaskell]], which aimed to highlight the social and economic pressures some people were under. The novel is unusual, in that it is not set in London, as is Dickens' usual wont, but the fictitious Victorian industrial town of Coketown. It has met mixed critical response from a diverse range of critics, such [[Author:F. R. Leavis|F. R. Leavis]], [[Author:George Bernard Shaw|George Bernard Shaw]], and [[Author:Thomas Babington Macaulay|Thomas Macaulay]]. This was usual for Dickens' treatment of trade unions, and the pessimism about the division between capitalistic millowners and the undervalued workers, after the Industrial Revolution, set in the Victorian era of Britain.''{{wikipediaref|Hard Times}}
